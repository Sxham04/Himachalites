About Us

We’re from Himachal. All of us.
We grew up on winding roads, misty rooftops, pine forests, and voices that carry through the hills.
Then we left.
We — Surya, Prachi, and Shubham — moved to Gurugram for work. We didn’t go with complaints in our pockets. We went with curiosity. And the city was kind to us. We learned. We grew. We built good things with good people.
But even on the busiest days, Himachal never left our minds. Surya missed the mountains every morning as he opened his laptop. I — Prachi — felt the urge to do something for Himachal and its people. I wanted to create something that made it easier for locals to stay local, and still thrive.
And that’s how Himachalites began — not with a pitch, but with a shared feeling that wouldn’t go away.
Surya is the founder. He’s an art director with 10 years of experience in visual storytelling, UI/UX design, and branding. He shaped how Himachalites looks — clean, grounded, and true to its roots.
I’m Prachi, the co-founder. I bring 9 years of experience in content writing, strategy, UX writing, SEO, copywriting, and digital marketing. I write what we want to say — in our videos, our captions, and everything in between.
Shubham, who worked as a graphic and UI/UX designer for 8 years, is the eye behind our camera. He captures Himachal not as a backdrop, but as a living, breathing place.
And slowly, our team grew.
Shivam helps gather stories that might otherwise slip through the cracks. He collects and manages information about local festivals, cultural events, and community traditions — the kind of things that make Himachal what it is. He also connects with people on the ground and helps explain what Himachalites stands for.
Soham is the developer who turned our idea into a working website. He built this space. Without him, none of it would live online.
So that’s us. A small team from Himachal, shaped by the city, pulled back by the hills — now working to tell stories that feel honest, quiet, and close to home.
This isn’t a brand. It’s a return.
